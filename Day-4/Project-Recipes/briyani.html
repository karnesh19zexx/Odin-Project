Briyani

Ingredients

  • Good quality rice, basmati
  • Masalas
  • Meat - prefered: Chicken/mutton/lamb

Procedure

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 20–30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pressure cooker or deep cooking pot.
  3. Add the whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, and cardamom) and sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chili, then sauté for about 1 minute.
  6. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  7. Add the mixed vegetables, turmeric powder, red chili powder, biryani masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  8. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the vegetable mixture. Stir gently to avoid breaking the rice grains.
  9. Pour in the required amount of water and mix everything together.
  10. Cover and cook until the rice is fully cooked and the water is absorbed. If using a pressure cooker, cook for 1 whistle on high heat and then 5 minutes on low heat.
  11. Allow the biryani to rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid.
  12. Garnish with fresh coriander and mint leaves.
  13. Serve hot with raita, curd, or your preferred side dish.
